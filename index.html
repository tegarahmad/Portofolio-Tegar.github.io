Forecasting Bitcoin Price Using Machine Learning and SHAP Interaction Value

Bitcoin has gained popularity as a cryptocurrency asset, attracting many investors due to its high price volatility. This study uses machine learning techniques—XGBoost, CatBoost, Random Forest, and LightGBM—and SHAP (SHapley Additive ExPlanation) interaction values to improve Bitcoin price forecasting accuracy. The dataset includes variables like opening price, closing price, and trading volume. The models are evaluated based on performance metrics, and SHAP values provide insights into the impact of each feature on price predictions. The results highlight the importance of feature relationships and offer transparency for better investment decisions in the cryptocurrency market.

Carbon Tax Judge Analysis with K-modes

Four clusters of respondents show diverse views on the carbon tax in Indonesia. Most agree that the carbon tax is effective in reducing emissions and has positive impacts on the environment, public health, and infrastructure development. However, they also highlight the need for more intensive public outreach, system improvements, tax incentives, and increased investment in clean energy technology. There are also differing opinions on the tax rates, its application across various sectors, and its impact on industries.
